Mechanical Watches

A mechanical watch is a watch that uses a clockwork mechanism to measure the passage of time. A mechanical watch is driven by a mainspring which has to be wound periodically. Its force is transmitted through a series of gears to power the balance wheel, a weighted wheel which oscillates back and forth at a constant rate. A device called an escapment releases the watch's wheels to move forward a small amount with each swing of the balance wheel, moving the watch's hands forward at a constant rate. This makes the "ticking" sound characteristic of all mechanical watches. Mechanical watches evolved in Europe in the 17th century from spring-powered clocks, which appeared in the 15th century. More information about mechanical watches can be found in the corresponding Wikipedia entry.

Components

The internal mechanism of a watch, excluding the face and hands, is called the movement which is composed of the following four parts:

Mainspring
Stores mechanical energy to power the watch.
Gear Train
Transmits the force of the mainspring to the balance wheel and adds up the swings of the balance wheel to get units of seconds, minutes and hours.
Balance Wheel
Device that oscillates back and forth, with each swing taking precisely the same amount of time. This is the timekeeping element in the watch.
Escapment
A mechanism that has the dual function of keeping the balance wheel vibrating by giving it a push with each swing, and allowing the watch's gears to advance or "escape" by a set amount with each swing. The periodic stopping of the gear train makes the ticking sound of a mechanical watch.

Complications

A watch's additional functions besides basic timekeeping are traditionally referred to as complications. Mechanical watches may have the following complications:

Complication Description
Automatic Winding A rotating weight mechanism is used to wind the watch's mainspring automatically using the natural motions of the wearer's wrist.
Chronograph A stopwatch function controlled by additional buttons on the watch case.
Repeater Chimes the hours audibly at the press of a button.
Tourbillon The balance wheel is mounted in a rotating cage so that it will experience all positions equally, theoretically eliminating gravitational bias.
